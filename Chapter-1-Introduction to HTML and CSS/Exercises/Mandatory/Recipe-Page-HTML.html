Sushi

Minutes: 45 | Rating: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ 2M reviews

Sushi is a Japanese dish of prepared vinegared rice (鮨飯, sushi-meshi), usually with some sugar and salt, plus a variety of ingredients (ねた, neta), such as vegetables, and any meat, but most commonly seafood (often raw but can be cooked).

Ingredients


Sushi Rice Nori (Seaweed) Fish
Vegetables Wasabi Soy Sauce

Method

  1. Rince and cook the rice

    2. Rinse rice with water and repeat 5-6 times. This softens the rice to create the Japanese sushi texture. Boil water and boil the rice according to the package instructions, using 2-3 times more water than rice. Add salt for taste.


  2. Cool and dry the rice

    3. To prepare rice, combine sugar and apple vinegar in a measuring glass and stir until the sugar dissolves. Gradually add the mixture to the dry rice, spreading it carefully with a spoon and gently stirring to ensure that the vinegar is well absorbed.


  3. Start shaping the rice

    4. Using clean hands, soak the rice in a bowl, knead it slightly longer, and compact it to obtain a cylindric form without losing thickness.


  4. Put rice on the algae

    5. Moisten rice in a bowl, knead slightly, and compact into a cylindrical shape. Roll nori algae on a bamboo mat, then spread a level layer on a solid surface, ensuring it travels freely without reducing thickness.


  5. Make the sushi roll

    6. Prepare a sushi roll by covering rice with mild sauce, topping with surimi sticks, avocado, or salmon, rolling on a bamboo mat, and cutting into equal pieces.


  6. Serve

    7. To finish we serve the maki and you can eat them with soy sauce and other complements, such as wasabi paste or ginger slices. You can also add a little sesame.
