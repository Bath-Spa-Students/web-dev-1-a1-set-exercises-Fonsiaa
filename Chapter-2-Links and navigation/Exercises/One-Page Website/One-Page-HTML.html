The Lion King

About

The Lion King is a 1994 American animated musical coming-of-age drama film directed by Roger Allers and Rob Minkoff, produced by Don Hahn. The film has an ensemble voice cast and is based on William Shakespeare's Hamlet, plus elements from the Biblical stories of Joseph and Moses. The plot follows a young heir apparent who is forced to leave when his uncle murders his father and seizes the kingdom. The film was initially, non-musical and more factual0 in style. The plot is based on African animals and follows a young heir apparent who returns to confront the usurper's oppressive authority. The Lion King was released on June 15, 1994, and received critical acclaim for its music, story, themes, and animation. It is the highest-grossing traditionally animated film of all time and the best-selling film on home video, selling over 55 million copies world wide.


Plot

The sun was raising and all the animals started gathering at the kingdom's Pride Land of Africa. There, was a cub named Simba, the son of King Mufasa and Queen Sarabai. Rafiki the mandrill, the kingdom's shaman and advisor, carefully lifted Simba to be praised. But the brother of King Mufasa, Scar had a different plan.

As Simba's youthful days has come, King Mufasa showed the Pride Land and told him that everything he saw is part of the circle of life. With Simba's amazement he want to see the elephant graveyard where his uncle Scar lived, with Nala, his best friend. Simba's thinks with his father as the king of the pride land, nothing will happen to them. Nala was terrified with Simba's action to go there but still when with him. With so little to no knowledge, they were spotted by the hyenas named Shenzi, Banzai, and Ed.

But at the end, the hyenas who once obeyed Scar has turned against him. With Scar and the hyenas gone, Simba is able to take his spot as the rightous King of the Pride Land, along side of the him is his best friend, Nala. With the Pride Land back to the good old days, the circle of life continues to live.
